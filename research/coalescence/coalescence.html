Effects of soot coalescence process on a non-premixed Jet A-1 coflow flame using a sectional particle dynamics model

Authors: Littin, M. , Demarco, R., Escudero, F., Lefevre, G., Yon, J., Mazur, M., & Fuentes, A.

Presented: April 2023, European Combustion Meeting (ECM) 2023

Abstract

A simple coalescence model is implemented in the CoFlame code to simulate a non-premixed CH4/Jet A-1 coflow sooting flame. Through a comparative analysis, this study aims to examine the effects of coalescence on soot production based on inception and surface growth via PAH adsorption and the hydrogen abstraction carbon addition (HACA) model. This approach modifies the surface growth dynamics of soot particles, affecting the surface area/volume ratio, decreasing the HACA mechanism and increasing PAH adsorption. The results show a decrease in the number of primary particles, observed all along the flame region. The coalescent model requires further improvements to evaluate the number of particles per aggregate. However, a better agreement with experimental data is observed than the default model, especially in the formation zone, where the coalescent model captures the magnitude and trend of the primary particle diameter.
